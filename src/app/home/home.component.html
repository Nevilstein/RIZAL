About José Rizal

José Rizal was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Philippines. While living in Europe, Rizal wrote about the discrimination that accompanied Spain's colonial rule of his country. He returned to the Philippines in 1892, but was exiled due to his desire for reform. Although he supported peaceful change, Rizal was convicted of sedition and executed on December 30, 1896, at age 35.

Major Periods in the Life of Rizal

First Period (1861-1872)

Period then Rizal learned how to read, write and listed to stories that triggered imaginative and critical thinking on his part.

The period when the following values and virtues were developed in him: industriousness, creativeness, rationality and dignity.

Second Period (1872-1882)

The first turning point in the life of Rizal, when at 11 years old, was enrolled at Ateneo Municipal.

It was the period when Frs. Gomez, Burgos and Zamora were unjustly executed by the Spanish government.

Third Period (1882-1892)

This is the time that Rizal decided to leave the Philippines to escape persecution. He went to Europe and became an observer and a student.

He urged the Filipino colony in Spain to prove that Filipinos can compete with the Europeans in intellect and talent. It is also during this period that he took part in Propaganda Movement, which is based in Europe.

Fourth Period (1892-1896)

The last period in the life of Rizal. Rizal was exiled in Dapitan, where he also demonstrated what an individual can do and accomplish within a short period of time.

It was here where he detached his connection with politics and devoted more of his time in practical service and usefulness to the community.
